Articulated Robotic Arm/Copy-Cat Arm

The project focuses on developing a life-sized, articulated robotic arm primarily constructed from wood, integrating high load-bearing servos to ensure robust functionality. This endeavor emphasizes a blend of mechanical and fabrication skills, including the precise cutting and working of wood to create stable mounts and brackets. The design process involves crafting a gripper assembly capable of delicate and firm handling, and CNC cutting of metal gears to construct a reliable drive system for the servos.

This robotic arm is designed with five degrees of freedom, enabling a wide range of motion and flexibility. It is powered by six high-torque servos, chosen for their ability to handle significant loads and provide precise control. The control system is centered around an Arduino UNO microcontroller, which allows for both manual operation and gesture-based control options, enhancing the user experience and providing versatility in interaction.

The integration of 3D printing technology in the construction of the arm highlights the project's innovative approach, combining traditional woodworking with modern fabrication techniques. This approach not only provides a sturdy structure but also allows for customization and intricate designs that would be challenging with wood alone.

This project offers a comprehensive hands-on experience in servo control and robotics, making it an excellent educational tool for those interested in the field. By bridging the gap between traditional craftsmanship and advanced robotics, the project demonstrates the potential for creative solutions in engineering and technology. The resulting robotic arm stands as a testament to the effective fusion of different materials and technologies to achieve a functional and educational robotic system.

PROJECT DETAILS:

Fig: Articulated Robotic ARM/Copy-Cat Arm


The project focuses on developing a life-sized articulated robotic arm, constructed primarily from wood and equipped with high load-bearing servos. This project involves a significant amount of mechanical and fabrication skill, including:

• Cutting and working with wood to create mounts and brackets.
• Designing the gripper assembly.
• CNC cutting of metal gears and the drive system for servos.

It is a 3D-printed robotic arm with five degrees of freedom, designed to provide hands-on experience in servo control and robotics. This arm is powered by six high-torque servos and controlled by an Arduino UNO microcontroller, offering both manual and gesture-based control options.

GOALS AND APPLICATIONS

The project aims to enhance understanding of robotic systems, combining practical mechanical design with advanced control systems. Whether you're a student, hobbyist, or professional, this project provides a thorough introduction to building and programming a functional robotic arm, making it ideal for both learning and experimentation. The robotic arm has a wide range of potential applications, including:

• Educational settings for teaching and training purposes.
• Prototyping and automation tasks in various industries.
• Research in human-robot interaction to advance the field.

• Assistive technology to support individuals with disabilities.

CONCLUSION

The life-sized articulated robotic arm project merges mechanical engineering and advanced control systems, offering a comprehensive learning experience in robotics. By utilizing wood for structural components, high load-bearing servos for movement, and an Arduino UNO microcontroller for control, the project encapsulates a broad spectrum of skills including mechanical fabrication, electronic integration, and programming.

The robotic arm, with its five degrees of freedom and advanced control options, serves as an invaluable educational tool, a prototype for automation tasks, a platform for research in human-robot interaction, and a potential aid in assistive technology. This project not only enhances the participants' technical skills but also contributes to broader applications in education, industry, research, and accessibility.
