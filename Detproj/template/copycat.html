Life Sized robotic Arm

The project focuses on developing a life-sized articulated robotic arm, constructed primarily from wood and equipped with high load-bearing servos. This project involves a significant amount of mechanical and fabrication skill, including:
• Cutting and working with wood to create mounts and brackets.
• Designing the gripper assembly.
• CNC cutting of metal gears and the drive system for servos.
It is a 3D-printed robotic arm with five degrees of freedom, designed to provide hands-on experience in servo control and robotics. This arm is powered by six high-torque servos and controlled by an Arduino UNO microcontroller, offering both manual and gesture-based control options.

ABOUT PROJECT:

Fig.1: Protoype of the Life Sized robotic Arm

The project involves creating a life-sized articulated robotic arm primarily from wood, utilizing high load-bearing servos. It requires significant mechanical and fabrication skills, such as cutting wood for mounts and brackets, designing a gripper assembly, and CNC cutting metal gears. The 3D-printed arm has five degrees of freedom, powered by six high-torque servos, and is controlled by an Arduino UNO microcontroller with manual and gesture-based options. Aimed at enhancing understanding of robotic systems, it serves as an educational tool for students, hobbyists, and professionals. Applications include education, prototyping, automation, human-robot interaction research, and assistive technology for individuals with disabilities.
The life-sized articulated robotic arm merges mechanical engineering and advanced control systems, enhancing skills in fabrication, electronics, and programming, with applications in education, automation, research, and assistive technology.
